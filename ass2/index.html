Global Distribution of the Restaurants

Paris stands out at the top, with the highest number of restaurants, followed closely by Bangkok and London. Cities like Tokyo, New York, and Mexico City have a moderate representation with around 4-5 restaurants. The chart displays a typical long-tail distribution, where a few cities have a high number of restaurants, and as we move down the chart, the number of restaurants per city decreases, with a vast majority having only one.

The majority of the restaurants have tasting menu prices ranging between 150 to 550 AUD. Only a few outliers have prices above or below this range. There isn't a strong direct correlation between rank and price. In conclusion, while rank might influence the tasting menu's price to some extent, other factors likely play a significant role in pricing decisions, as evidenced by the varied prices across similar ranks.
